# Noodle Stories Collective

## Overview

The **Noodle Stories Collective** is an anti-authoritarian poetry and mutual-aid circle operating out of [Noodle Stories](../Locations/Noodle-Stories.md), a small noodle shop in Nashville's arts district. Publicly it is a loose arts scene. Privately it is a trusted neighborhood network for people who need food, warnings, and language sharp enough to survive pressure.

## Public Face

- Hosts poetry nights, zine swaps, small benefit readings, and noisy post-show meals.
- Publishes cheap broadsides and hand-folded chapbooks under rotating titles.
- Presents itself as artsy, cranky, local, and too disorganized to be worth official attention.
- Welcomes broke artists, students, dishwashers, street musicians, and anyone who can behave in the room.

## Actual Function

- Mutual-aid hub for artists, service workers, and neighborhood dissidents.
- Rumor filter for abuses by landlords, cops, corps, federal agents, and political fixers.
- Soft message network using poems, event flyers, menu specials, and spoken-word refrains as deniable signals.
- Cultural pressure valve: the collective turns fear into jokes, jokes into poems, and poems into public memory.

## Assets / Reach

- The noodle shop's dining room, back room, kitchen, service corridor access, and after-hours reading schedule.
- A small but loyal audience across Nashville's arts district.
- Zine distribution through galleries, clubs, coffee counters, laundromats, and rehearsal spaces.
- Informal watchers who notice raids, harassment, suspicious vans, and sudden property-interest shifts.
- Enough local affection that direct pressure against the shop may create more noise than it solves.

## Relationships

- Based at [Noodle Stories](../Locations/Noodle-Stories.md).
- Part of Nashville's broader arts-district and underground mutual-aid ecosystem.
- Sympathetic to anti-corporate and anti-federal local resistance, but not automatically aligned with any runner crew, gang, or political faction.

## Open Questions

- Who inside the collective is willing to cross from poetry and aid into operational support?
- Which local authorities already know the collective is more than an arts circle?
- What favor would earn enough trust to use the back room safely?
