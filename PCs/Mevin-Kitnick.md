# Mevin Kitnick

## Overview

**Mevin Kitnick** is an active player character, played by **Clint**, and the crew's primary **corpo decker**. He fills the backline Matrix/technical lane: host intrusion, data analysis, corporate-procedure reads, dossier skepticism, Matrix legwork, and coordination with **Cindy Lou Jenkins** as a semi-autonomous Matrix partner.

He reads as a working shadowrunner with real corporate-security experience rather than a pure street punk. His useful edge is not just that he can deck; it is that he understands how corporate systems fail, how internal access gets abused, and when a dossier is telling the truth badly on purpose.

## Character Profile

- **Role:** Decker / Matrix investigator / technical backline
- **Player:** Clint
- **Style:** cautious corporate-procedural analyst with enough shadow experience to mistrust clean paperwork
- **Crew tags:** **AI Enthusiast**, **Backline Combatant**, **Hoarder**
- **Known iconography:** Condor / screaming birdman icon, per current deck-tool defaults
- **Operational tendency:** prefers due diligence, source-checking, and reading system context before committing to a noisy intrusion

## Campaign Role

Mevin is usually the crew member most responsible for:

- host intrusion and Matrix legwork
- reading corporate access patterns
- identifying when a data problem is probably an internal compromise
- interpreting suspicious gaps in dossiers or monitoring records
- managing the crew's relationship with Matrix-adjacent tools, paydata, and utility recovery
- coordinating Cindy Lou's Matrix/social-pattern support during live runs

## Known Facts

- Mevin Kitnick is played by **Clint**.
- He is a **corpo decker**.
- Key crew tags: **AI Enthusiast**, **Backline Combatant**, **Hoarder**.
- He is the crew member with the main relationship to **Herrick**, the Nashville PD shadowrun liaison.

- His corporate-security background remains directly useful in play; during the **2026-06-04** Griswell job, he was the first to identify the likelihood of an **internal access compromise** rather than a simple one-man leak.
- During the **2026-07-09** Pixel Sticks job intake, Mevin recognized that the target utilities sounded like military-grade Matrix zero-days: valuable if recovered cleanly, but likely degraded if spammed in public attacks.
- He also noticed suspicious gaps and edits in the Johnson's monitoring dossier, suggesting the client or source may have withheld relevant activity.
- At Happy Cat, Mevin jacked into the local Matrix through a public access point and brought Valgaut along through a crude tethered connection while Kurgan remained outside as meatspace overwatch.
- Mevin hid the real quality of his cyberdeck well enough that it read more like a high-end cyberterminal than an obvious illegal deck.

## Matrix Style

Mevin's Matrix play style, as established so far, emphasizes:

- **reading the setup before diving deep** — he looks for gaps, omissions, and access-pattern clues;
- **corporate procedure literacy** — he knows what normal permissions and monitoring should look like;
- **operational deniability** — he has reason to keep his better gear from being obvious;
- **tool recovery awareness** — he understands that pristine utilities and burned utilities are very different prizes;
- **backline support** — he coordinates with meatspace overwatch rather than treating decking as a solo stunt.
