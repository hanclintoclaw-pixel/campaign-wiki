# Session 2026-05-07

## Summary

The crew opened by recapping the expanding Darla-led influence operation. The major actionable leads from the prior investigation were confirmed as a central warehouse used to receive and redistribute tainted promo goods, plus the still-unresolved name Mother Yahweh. The team also kept the original SC Music objective in view: identifying who actually stole Cindy Lou’s master recordings, even as the snack-distribution conspiracy kept widening.

Pursuing the contaminated-goods angle, the runners examined the persuasion mechanism more closely. The adulterated Meatlets appeared to act like one half of a binary poison: eating them increased suggestibility, which then let a separate subliminal audio/message source steer behavior. Valgaut failed a willpower check and briefly developed an urge to deliver tuna to 612 Wharf Avenue, plus a bizarre but memorable fixation on aquatic-animal voting rights, confirming that the influence pipeline could push targeted actions without obvious overt magic.

That address led the team to a run-down warehouse site. From surveillance and distant observation, they identified Ace Freelits gangers doing manual labor, boxing and shipping counterfeit Bebop Cola and Meatlets, and preparing to cover Mingus posters with new Bebop Cola posters for the upcoming Darla-Doo release. The building appeared to be drawing heavy power, likely from a diesel generator rather than city lines, and at least twenty surveillance drones were seen returning to a roof charging station. The session ended with the crew debating whether to wait out departing workers or risk approaching a site that could still cover its roofline with shotgun fire.

## Major Scenes

- The group reviewed prior discoveries and reaffirmed the key leads: Mother Yahweh and a central redistribution warehouse.
- The team tested how the tainted promo pipeline works and confirmed that the contaminated Meatlets increase susceptibility to subliminal influence.
- Valgaut received a compelled urge tied to tuna delivery and the address 612 Wharf Avenue, linking the subliminal effect directly to a real-world destination.
- The crew traveled to the warehouse, hid Grandpa from overhead surveillance, and conducted remote observation with drones and optics.
- Surveillance identified Ace Freelits labor, counterfeit snack repackaging, poster-swap activity, heavy drone traffic, and a roof charging station.

## NPCs Introduced / In Play

- Mother Yahweh (still unresolved, but reinforced as a live lead)
- Ace Freelits (low-rent motorcycle gang working the warehouse as hired labor)

## Clues Gained

- The contaminated Meatlets are part of a two-part influence mechanism: the food increases suggestibility, and a separate subliminal signal carries the actual instruction.
- Valgaut’s compelled instruction pointed to 612 Wharf Avenue, which matches the warehouse address already tied to the counterfeit-goods pipeline.
- The warehouse is actively repackaging and shipping makeshift artificial Bebop Cola and Meatlets.
- After the snack shipments, workers were set to collect posters and cover Mingus posters with new Bebop Cola posters for the upcoming Darla-Doo release.
- Ace Freelits personnel are visibly involved at the warehouse, apparently as low-level contract labor.
- The site supports significant surveillance infrastructure: many light drones were operating in the area, and at least twenty returned to a charging station on the roof.
- The building likely relies on an internal diesel generator or other nonstandard heavy power source rather than ordinary city power.

## Decisions Made

- Treat the warehouse lead as the crew’s immediate next operational focus.
- Use stealth, remote observation, and drones rather than rolling Grandpa directly up to the target.
- Consider waiting for more workers and drones to clear before making a closer approach.
- Continue treating Mother Yahweh and the tainted-promotional-goods pipeline as central parts of the broader Darla operation.

## Changes to Campaign State

- The previously abstract central warehouse lead is now tied to a specific address: 612 Wharf Avenue.
- The team has direct evidence that the warehouse is an active packaging and redistribution node for counterfeit Darla/Bebop/Meatlets promo goods.
- The contaminated-snack threat is now better understood as a binary influence system combining ingestion with subliminal messaging.
- Ace Freelits are now concretely linked to the warehouse operation, at least as disposable labor.
