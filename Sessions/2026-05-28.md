# Session 2026-05-28

## Summary

With the warehouse fight over, the session shifted from discovery and breach into **aftermath management**. The crew now had a knocked-out **Mother Yahweh**, a dead insect spirit, an effectively broken warehouse line, and a still-active **Core 7 / Site 7** installation in the woods. Instead of charging straight into another fight, they spent most of the session working through the practical and strategic consequences of what they had uncovered.

The biggest question was no longer whether **Core 7** existed, but what to do with it. The runners increasingly treated the site not as a simple hostile AI nest but as a cruelly maintained **wetware system** built around a captive cybered dolphin and a cluster of damaged awakened aquatic support creatures. Discussion turned toward whether the site could be taken over, stabilized, or repurposed long enough to keep the creatures alive, and whether **Taco's** property might serve as a future fallback habitat if extraction ever became possible.

At the same time, the session pushed the music-industry thread forward in a major way. The crew's reasoning tied **Core 7**, **SC Music**, and older **Swiftwing Records** knowbot architecture more tightly together. By session logic, some portion of the code, design, or commercializable behavior around Core 7 appears to have been copied, licensed, or otherwise monetized into later **Cindy**-related software systems. That raised the stakes immediately: if the truth comes out, **SC Music** may be heading for a major public scandal rather than merely a stolen-masters dispute.

The session ended with the operational crisis still unresolved but the next phase becoming clearer: the warehouse side had been disrupted, the site itself remained usable but morally intolerable, and the crew had begun moving from "what is this place?" to "how do we safely break, expose, or salvage it without making things worse?" A short aftermath timeskip then pushed the campaign forward by **three days**, establishing the new active in-world date as **2066-05-04**.

## Major Scenes

- The group regrouped in the wake of the **612 Wharf Avenue** breach and inventoried the immediate scene: a dead insect spirit, an unconscious **Mother Yahweh**, and a warehouse no longer functioning as before.
- The runners talked through whether to simply walk away, destroy more evidence, or try to **hold / control the site** long enough to manage the fallout.
- Discussion focused on the condition of the dolphin node and the **hurricane seals**, reinforcing that the visible Site 7 ecosystem had been keeping badly compromised creatures alive for years rather than functioning as a clean automated facility.
- The team increasingly treated **Core 7** as a captive wetware intelligence with practical value, moral weight, and deep historical significance rather than merely as a target.
- The crew explored the idea of keeping Core 7 functioning in place temporarily versus relocating the dolphin to a safer environment.
- **Taco** emerged as a practical logistics anchor in that discussion, with Curtis later spending time preparing a possible **dolphin landing / habitat space** at Taco's place.
- The session's matrix / continuity reasoning connected **Core 7** more directly to the older **Swiftwing / Cindy** thread, with Core 7 read as a semi-autonomous knowbot-like system whose architecture may have been commercially copied into later software descendants.
- The implications of that link pushed **SC Music** into a more dangerous position: not just a harmed stakeholder in the Darla/stolen-masters problem, but a possible corporate node tied to technology derived from a grotesque buried predecessor system.
- The session closed with an explicit **three-day aftermath timeskip**, carrying the active campaign date forward to **2066-05-04** and showing the crew beginning practical prep for the next phase rather than resolving the whole Site 7 problem on the spot.
- That montage advanced the campaign by **three days**, making **2066-05-04** the new current in-world date.

## Clues Gained

- **Core 7** may represent an earlier or underlying form of the same broad technological family that later surfaced through **Swiftwing Records** and Cindy's knowbot architecture.
- At least some commercially useful layer of that architecture appears to have been **copied, licensed, bought, or otherwise extracted** from the Core 7 lineage rather than invented cleanly from scratch.
- **SC Music** could be exposed to a much larger scandal than a simple artist-management or stolen-recordings problem if that history becomes public.
- The Site 7 creatures appear to have been kept alive in visibly compromised conditions for **years**, reinforcing that the site is both technically functional and morally grotesque.
- The dolphin node may be too valuable, too dangerous, or too fragile for a simple smash-and-grab solution.
- The crew's current problem has expanded from a warehouse/counterfeit-goods disruption into a **containment, rescue, and exposure problem** touching older campaign mysteries.

## Decisions Made

- Treat the warehouse aftermath as a strategic decision point rather than immediately charging into another violent escalation.
- Seriously consider **taking control of Site 7's surviving infrastructure** rather than simply abandoning it.
- Treat the dolphin node as something that may need **stabilization and eventual relocation**, not immediate destruction.
- Begin practical thought toward using **Taco's** property as possible support infrastructure for a future extraction/rescue attempt.
- Continue following the **SC Music / Swiftwing / Cindy** connection as a central line of inquiry, not a side mystery.

- **SC Music** has shifted from possible client-side victim to possible participant in a much uglier historical technology chain.
