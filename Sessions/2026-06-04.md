# Session 2026-06-04

## Summary

With the immediate **612 Wharf / Core 7** firefight behind them, the crew opened in a lighter aftermath mode. Curtis had been staying busy on the planned dolphin-and-hurricane-seal habitat while continuing fish deliveries to **Core 7**, and the group briefly checked in on character advancement, downtime, and what the next run should look like.

That next run arrived through **Ivan Toklas** at **Atlas's**. A generic **Mr. Johnson** representing **Griswell Data Services** hired the team to identify who low-level data analyst **Alvin Flang** was secretly working with. Alvin was suspected of skimming or reselling client data, but the payout and dossier details immediately suggested something uglier than a lone office idiot making side money. **Mevin's** corporate read pointed toward a real espionage problem and likely internal compromise inside Griswell itself.

The rest of the session played as a careful surveillance operation. The runners scoped Alvin's apartment, confirmed he was living above his means, found his absurd new sports car stashed behind a shabby building, and ruled out an obvious magical taint on Alvin or his immediate companion. The next day, they followed him to **Music Circle**, where he met with a protective ganger named **Diego** while a second, much cleaner-looking corporate watcher observed from across the park. That watcher used a tiny **spider drone**, was shadowed by two overhead surveillance drones, and then peeled away toward a waiting **driverless classic car**. The crew chose not to burn the tail at the last second, ending the session with the mystery watcher still unidentified and Curtis preparing to continue the follow from a safer distance.

## Major Scenes

- The session opened with downtime catch-up, including Curtis's ongoing dolphin-habitat work and continued fish deliveries to **Core 7** and the hurricane seals.
- **Ivan Toklas** called the crew to **Atlas's** for a new job briefing with a corporate **Mr. Johnson**.
- The Johnson identified **Alvin Flang**, a low-level **Griswell Data Services** analyst, as the bait in a suspected data-theft / corporate-espionage problem.
- The team learned Alvin had recently been spending far above his salary, including a flashy sports car, expensive wine, and other high-end purchases.
- **Mevin** concluded the problem probably ran deeper than Alvin freelancing: somebody inside Griswell likely gave him access he should not have had.
- The crew conducted first-pass surveillance at Alvin's apartment, where Curtis's drone work confirmed Alvin was home and hiding an out-of-place luxury car nearby.
- **Valgaut** got close enough for a magical read and found no awakened signature on Alvin or his immediate associate, only a faint unrelated magical presence somewhere higher above the building.
- The next day, the runners followed Alvin to the downtown lunch district at **Music Circle**.
- Alvin met with **Diego**, a hard-looking ganger/cybersam in **Heavenly Host** colors, while a separate polished corporate observer took position across the plaza.
- Kurgan spotted a tiny **spider drone** moving between the parties; later he saw the drone return to the observer, confirming that the generic corpo was running surveillance too.
- When the watcher broke contact, the team tailed him instead of Alvin and Diego, only to discover he also had two overhead surveillance drones and a waiting **driverless classic car**.
- The runners chose to preserve cover, gather what they could, and continue the investigation later rather than forcing an interception at the curb.

## Clues Gained

- **Griswell Data Services** appears to believe Alvin is selling client data, but the job details imply they are really trying to identify the downstream buyer.
- **Mevin's** analysis suggests Alvin probably did **not** obtain all of his access on his own; someone inside Griswell may have elevated his permissions.
- Alvin has been spending far above his means for roughly **six weeks**, making the payoff pipeline recent and visible.
- The likely exchange method is a **physical handoff**, not open Matrix transfer, during Alvin's Tuesday/Thursday lunch windows.
- Alvin is not obviously awakened or insect-tainted, at least from Valgaut's direct magical read.
- **Diego** appears to be there as muscle or confidence support rather than as the real brains of the operation.
- The separate corporate observer was conducting his own close surveillance with a **miniature spider drone**, suggesting Alvin and Diego were also being watched by someone besides the crew.
- The observer's extraction path involved **two airborne surveillance drones** and a **driverless classic car**, implying a better-resourced surveillance team than the one Alvin himself seemed to control.
- **Heavenly Host** insignia reappeared on Diego even though the gang was believed to be an old, largely extinct Matrix-oriented youth faction.

## Decisions Made

- Accept the **Griswell Data Services** contract through Ivan and the Johnson.
- Treat Alvin as **bait**, not as the final target.
- Prioritize surveillance over immediate snatch-and-grab tactics.
- Scope Alvin's apartment and habits before committing to the lunch-window tail.
- Let **Valgaut** check for magical contamination because of the crew's recent insect-spirit experience.
- Focus on the **mystery watcher** once it became clear someone else was also surveilling the meet.
- Avoid spooking the operation by jamming drones, hijacking Alvin's car, or forcing a confrontation at the last minute.
- End the session with the tail preserved and the follow-up handed to later observation rather than a rushed intervention.
