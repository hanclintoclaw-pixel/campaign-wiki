# Session 2026-07-23

## Summary

The crew breached the **21st-floor Pixel Sticks space** inside the [Vernon Winfrey Arkoblock](../Locations/Vernon-Winfrey-Arkoblock.md), first by trying a decker-social bluff and then by force. **Mevin** used Cindy Lou's quick host read to get insider handles, talked his way into the controlled floor, and then coordinated a hard breach when **Highbrow** refused the warning. **Valgaut** cut down a guard, **Kurgan** managed the room-clearing and hostage pressure, **Curtis** and Mevin found a dangerous scorpion-like combat drone with an expensive robot brain, and the crew recovered enough used utilities to satisfy the Johnson while Mevin retained encrypted material for later blue-book work with Cindy.

The run concluded with the crew fleeing in a repaired elevator while the scorpion drone fought [Chunky Sparkles](../NPCs/Chunky-Sparkles.md) and the young woman from Happy Cat. The crew received the full **60,000¥** payout, split **15,000¥ each**, and the GM awarded **7 Karma each**.

## Major Scenes

- The session resumed with the crew stacked at the 21st-floor steel security door after the prior stairwell climb and barricade bypass.
- Mevin listened through the door with augmented hearing and picked up nearby people plus the sound of heavy Matrix hardware or server racks.
- Kurgan tested the handle quietly and found the door unsecured enough to open without immediately forcing it.
- The crew debated a loud breach, a knock, or a deception play, with concern that gunfire could pull attention from other floors.
- Mevin asked Cindy Lou to use the Decker Experience / Happy Cat host context to pull a cover-story edge from the Pixel Sticks member-list cache.
- Cindy identified the useful angle as a trusted route-runner / route-board switchback / dead-drop authorization cover; the GM supplied handles including **Handy-Sandy**, **Highbrow**, and **Pop Onomata**.
- Mevin entered first, acting like expected Matrix-side traffic and asking for Handy-Sandy or Highbrow while Kurgan, Curtis, and Valgaut followed as his muscle.
- The floor presented as a painted-up gang/decker hideout with cameras, electronics, controlled access, and a virtual icon speaking through display hardware.
- Highbrow appeared as a youthful orc-barbarian Matrix icon on a screen and initially let only Mevin and one escort pass deeper.
- Mevin attempted to warn Highbrow that the military-grade utilities were hot, reckless, and attracting dangerous attention; Highbrow rejected the warning and threatened to have the crew thrown out.
- The crew pivoted to a synchronized assault: the front-line fighters hit the guards while Mevin used the distraction to rush the decker nest.
- Mevin used his shock hand on the hostile decker, dropping or nearly dropping him out of effective action rather than killing him.
- Valgaut cut one surprised guard in half with his axe during the breach.
- Kurgan and Valgaut forced a heavy inner door after the initial guard fight.
- The crew interrogated the surviving decker / Pixel Sticks member and learned the cell was not sitting on a large cash reserve; big pay depended on unfinished future work, while earlier proceeds had already been paid out or spent.
- The crew recovered or took control of the used utilities and local deck/host material connected to the Pixel Sticks operation.
- In a back room, they found the upper-floor host terminal tied to the laser array, confirming Mevin's theory that the upper host was nested from within the Happy Cat host and reachable through Happy Cat's Matrix path.
- Curtis identified a large scorpion-like combat drone with jaws-of-life style claws, SMGs, an assault-cannon tail, and a high-end robot brain.
- The drone's control list included **Ernie Pollock**, **Ziggy Hill**, **Lizzo Wells**, and two goons, one of whom had already been killed.
- Curtis found that ripping out the command harness could leave the drone brain-dead, while simply pulling radio/contact hardware risked uncontrolled behavior.
- Mevin and Curtis used the control interface to add the four runners to the drone's whitelist before triggering its violent autonomous mode.
- As the crew was finishing the drone work, Lizzo Wells arrived with Chunky Sparkles and saw the breached space.
- The crew used the drone as cover, repaired or reactivated a sealed elevator, and fled while the drone exchanged fire and electrical/magical attacks with Chunky Sparkles.
- The elevator descended with music playing as gunfire and electricity filled the upper-floor hallway.
- The crew exited through lower commercial floors with little immediate public attention.
- The Johnson-side handoff paid the full **60,000¥**: **40,000¥** for disrupting the Pixel Sticks threat plus the **20,000¥** utility-recovery bonus.
- Mevin argued that handing over the utilities the gang had actually been using satisfied the contract, while he retained encrypted material from the other room for later cracking with Cindy Lou.
- News later reported a major explosion and shootout at the arkoblock, but no destroyed drone wreck or Chunky Sparkles corpse was publicly reported.

## NPCs Introduced / In Play

- **Highbrow** - Pixel Sticks decker/leader voice for the 21st-floor cell; appeared through an orc-barbarian Matrix icon and tried to threaten the crew off.
- **Handy-Sandy** - Pixel Sticks handle recovered as a usable insider-reference name.
- **Pop Onomata** - Pixel Sticks handle recovered as a usable insider-reference name.
- **Ernie Pollock** - one of the drone-whitelisted Pixel Sticks-associated kids.
- **Ziggy Hill** - one of the drone-whitelisted Pixel Sticks-associated kids.
- **Lizzo Wells** - young woman from Happy Cat, later identified on the drone whitelist; arrived with Chunky Sparkles during the crew's escape.
- **Chunky Sparkles** - arrived during the endgame and fought the scorpion combat drone.
- **Pixel Sticks scorpion drone** - high-end autonomous combat drone with a dangerous whitelist / go-nuts mode.

## Clues Gained

- The 21st-floor Pixel Sticks cell had physical infrastructure, guards, cameras, and Matrix hardware, not just a loose youth-gang hangout.
- The upper-floor host / terminal was tied to the Happy Cat laser array and appeared nested through the Happy Cat host path.
- Pixel Sticks members had enough high-end support to operate or receive a military-grade scorpion combat drone, but not necessarily enough control to fully own it safely.
- The cell's big payday depended on unfinished work; the crew did not find a large nuyen reserve sitting on-site.
- The used utilities were recoverable enough to satisfy the Johnson-side handoff.
- Mevin retained encrypted utility/paydata material from the other room that may be worth more than the official recovery bonus if cracked safely.
- The scorpion drone's whitelist included local Pixel Sticks-associated names and now likely includes the crew's biometric profiles.
- Chunky Sparkles is powerful enough to stand against the scorpion drone at least briefly, using electrical or magical protection/attacks.
- The public aftermath was a major arkoblock incident, but the final status of Chunky Sparkles and the drone remains unconfirmed.

## Decisions Made

- The crew chose a deception-first entry rather than immediately shooting through the 21st-floor door.
- Mevin used Cindy Lou / Decker Experience support to extract useful Pixel Sticks handle names for the bluff.
- When Highbrow rejected the warning, the crew chose a hard synchronized breach.
- Mevin prioritized disabling the active decker and preserving utility access over simply destroying all equipment.
- Curtis and Mevin altered the scorpion drone's whitelist instead of trying to permanently disable it.
- The crew triggered the drone as escape-cover rather than staying to finish the fight with Chunky Sparkles and Lizzo Wells.
- The crew delivered the used utilities to claim the full bonus, while Mevin kept encrypted material for follow-up work with Cindy Lou.

## Rewards / Tallies

- **Karma:** 7 Karma each to Mevin, Valgaut, Kurgan, and Curtis.
- **Nuyen:** 60,000¥ total run payout, split 15,000¥ each.
- **Retained asset / open blue-book item:** Mevin retained encrypted utility/paydata material for possible later cracking with Cindy Lou.

## Changes to Campaign State

- The **Pixel Sticks / Military-Grade Utilities Run** is resolved as a paid run: the threat was disrupted and the Johnson paid the full recovery bonus.
- The 21st-floor Pixel Sticks cell is broken, but not cleanly contained; survivors, hidden sponsors, and fallout remain open.
- The Vernon Winfrey arkoblock now has a public explosion/shootout aftermath.
- The scorpion combat drone and Chunky Sparkles both remain unresolved after the upper-floor fight.
- The crew may have created a future problem by putting their biometric data onto the drone whitelist.
- Mevin now has encrypted material tied to the advanced utilities as a follow-up Matrix research hook.
