# Session 2026-06-25

## Summary

This was a **partial-party canon interlude**: a one-off, out-of-sequence session for **Kurgan** and **Curtis** only, backdated to occur before the current **Griswell Data Services** surveillance run. The table explicitly framed it as a side story filling in Kurgan's efforts to build a working relationship with **Mayor Mike Haggar** and **Joseph Neumann** after the anti-insect-spirit mayoral arc.

Joseph brought Kurgan, with Curtis as backup, into a direct but deniable mayoral problem. **Righteous Haze**, real name **Darrell Haysen**, had once been a respected Nashville shadow operator and had spent years as a fixer/security consultant for the mayor's outside apparatus. He had become complacent, expensive, unreliable, and dangerous. When the mayor tried to retire him from the organization, Haysen threatened to expose years of dirt on the mayor and city operations.

Kurgan and Curtis accepted the job as Kurgan's practical interview for replacing Haze's failed outside-security structure. They observed Haysen at **Josie's Billiard Hall**, confirmed that he had gone badly to seed, manipulated his usual pickup routine through Joseph, and convinced him to accept a ride in **Grandpa** after his expected city-arranged car failed to arrive. Once isolated, they killed him, stripped what useful gear and data they could, dumped the body far from town, ransacked his apartment, and left his distinctive cyberhand nailed to Josie's door as a change-of-management warning.

The most important discovery was not the outdated cyberware or cash, but Haysen's **data bank**. It contained records of jobs he had pulled for the mayor and several other senior city officials, including work he had apparently done behind the mayor's back. Kurgan and Curtis chose to keep that paydata between themselves rather than turn it over, giving Kurgan a powerful private lever as he moves deeper into Haggar's organization.

## Major Scenes

- The GM framed the session as a backfilled side story during the middle of the main run, focused on Kurgan's attempt to ingratiate himself with the mayor's team.
- **Joseph Neumann** contacted Kurgan with an opportunity to do a favor directly for **Mayor Haggar**.
- Kurgan called in **Curtis** as the only available teammate, making this a two-PC operation.
- Joseph and the mayor briefed them on **Righteous Haze / Darrell Haysen**, a retired shadow operator turned mayoral fixer/security consultant.
- The mayor explained that Haysen had been taking large payments while producing little useful work, then threatened blackmail when the mayor tried to remove him.
- Kurgan recognized Haze as an old Nashville underworld name from an earlier generation.
- The crew reviewed the dossier and concluded that Haysen was decadent, addicted, gambling heavily, and probably more sloppy than masterful.
- Kurgan and Curtis took **Grandpa** to Haysen's regular hangout, **Josie's Billiard Hall**, and watched his habits rather than immediately confronting him.
- They observed Haysen drinking imported **Pyramid / Pharaoh beer**, loan-sharking bar patrons, leaning on hangers-on, taking pills, and becoming steadily drunker.
- Curtis worked his way into Haysen's good graces with drinks and submarine stories while Kurgan stayed sober and kept watch.
- Kurgan called Joseph and had Haysen's normal city-arranged pickup canceled / rerouted.
- When Haysen's pickup call went unanswered, Curtis offered him a ride in Grandpa, and Haysen accepted alone.
- After the pickup, Kurgan and Curtis isolated and killed Haysen, then drove far outside the city to dispose of the body.
- They stripped Haysen's cheap pistol, outdated cyberware parts, and most importantly his data bank.
- They ransacked Haysen's upscale apartment and came away with additional cash and poor-quality old gear.
- Kurgan nailed Haysen's distinctive cyberhand to Josie's door as a grisly signal that management had changed.
- Kurgan reported back to Joseph that the "frustrations" had ended, and Joseph indicated the mayor looked forward to further developing the relationship.

## Clues Gained

- **Righteous Haze** had once been a real Nashville underworld figure, but by 2066 he had become sloppy, addicted, under-maintained, and dependent on mayoral money.
- Haggar's outside fixer/security apparatus had become rotten enough that the mayor wanted it rebuilt from the ground up.
- Haysen's threat was not only personal memory: he kept a **physical / portable data bank** with records of mayoral and city-official shadow work.
- Haysen had apparently been doing work for several other senior city officials behind the mayor's back.
- Much of Haysen's cyberware was outdated or nonfunctional because he had not maintained it.
- Kurgan now has a private cache of politically useful paydata that can accelerate his future mayoral-security role.

## Decisions Made

- Kurgan accepted the job as a way to prove himself useful to the mayor's organization.
- Curtis joined as Kurgan's backup and took a practical, low-politics role in the operation.
- The two avoided a direct public fight, choosing observation, pickup manipulation, and isolation instead.
- They used Joseph to prevent Haysen's normal driver from arriving.
- They killed Haysen after luring him into Grandpa and disposed of the body outside town.
- They kept the recovered paydata for themselves rather than giving it to the mayor or the rest of the crew.
- They destroyed the disposable disguises used during the operation.
- Kurgan left Haysen's distinctive cyberhand on Josie's door as a warning to Haysen's hangers-on.
