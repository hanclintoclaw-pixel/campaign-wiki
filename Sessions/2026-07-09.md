# Session 2026-07-09

## Summary

The crew moved from the concluded **Griswell / Palermo** surveillance job into a new Matrix-focused contract routed through **Ivan** at **Atlas's**. Nashville's shadow economy is running hot in the wake of the broader Cross / Princeps corporate pressure, and Atlas's was nearly empty because most local runners were already working. Ivan introduced the team to an unusually anonymous, high-end **Mr. Johnson**, who offered **40,000¥** to stop a Matrix youth gang from disrupting Nashville's telecom grid, plus a **20,000¥** bonus if the crew recovered the state-of-the-art attack utilities the gang had been using. The sparse dossier pointed toward **Happy Cat Konpeki and Collectibles**, where the team began street and Matrix legwork and ended the session with **Mevin**, **Valgaut**, and **Cindy Lou Jenkins** entering the local Matrix while **Kurgan** watched the meatspace crowd.

## Major Scenes

- The session opened with campaign context: **Claude August**, **The Princeps Group**, **Cross Applied Technology**, and other corporate actors remain part of a broader regional pressure environment, even though the current job is not yet confirmed to belong to any one of them.
- **Kurgan** and **Valgaut** confirmed they know the public rumors and private truth that Claude August, CEO of Princeps, is secretly a dragon; the team also recalled that older Cross / Princeps technology disputes were never fully unraveled.
- The GM established that the Nashville shadow scene is busier than usual: Matrix noise is up, magic-sensitive characters can feel more strange activity than usual, and Atlas's is nearly empty because runners are already out on contracts.
- **Ivan** sent the team upstairs to meet a very polished, very forgettable **Mr. Johnson**.
- The Johnson presented a thin dossier on a troublesome Matrix youth gang using outside-supplied, bleeding-edge utilities to vandalize and disrupt Nashville telecom systems.
- The job offer was **40,000¥** to make the gang stop being a Nashville Matrix problem, with an additional **20,000¥** if the crew could recover the advanced utilities instead of merely destroying or neutralizing them.
- **Mevin** recognized that the utilities were the Matrix equivalent of military-grade zero-days: potentially very valuable when pristine, but rapidly degraded if spammed across active targets.
- The crew inferred that the low recovery bounty might mean the utilities are already burned, that the client mostly wants proof of disposal, or that the client is hiding how personally connected they are to the software.
- Mevin noticed suspicious gaps and edits in the dossier's monitoring record, suggesting the Johnson's side may have withheld or redacted activity that should have been visible.
- With help from Cindy Lou's background Matrix/social-pattern read, Mevin identified the likely tag attached to the recent vandalism wave: **the Pixel Sticks**.
- Mevin checked with Ivan before leaving. Ivan treated the job as a valuable but plausible milk run, said this was a client he would like to see happy, and did not flag the Johnson as a special burn risk.
- Following Cindy's advice, Kurgan checked for tails before the crew left Atlas's; he found no sign that anyone had clocked them.
- The team left casually, took a circuitous route, and successfully shook any hypothetical surveillance before heading toward East Nashville.
- The lead brought them to **Happy Cat Konpeki and Collectibles**, a bright convenience-store / collectibles shop in a crowded arkoblock district.
- Street observation revealed a dense teen scene full of corporate-logo fashion, poseur gang colors, possible decker kids, plugged-in SINless locals, and enough public Matrix traffic to make identifying the Pixel Sticks difficult.
- **Valgaut** sensed a fairly powerful awakened animal inside Happy Cat, but could not identify the species before the session ended.
- **Mevin** jacked into the local Matrix through a nearby public access point, bringing Valgaut in through a crude tethered connection while Kurgan remained outside as meatspace overwatch.
- Mevin concealed the true quality of his cyberdeck well enough to read as a high-end cyberterminal rather than an obvious illegal deck.
- The local Matrix appeared as a 1990s-cyberspace style grid of small-business hosts, advertising icons, and local traffic, including the [Happy Cat Public Storefront Host](../Tech/Matrix/Happy-Cat-Public-Storefront-Host.md).
- Mevin asked Cindy Lou to help survey the crowd and build social profiles; Cindy's familiar performer-icon appeared beside the team in the local Matrix.
- The session paused before the crew chose whether to inspect Happy Cat's host, scan the crowd icons, or pursue another lead.

## NPCs Introduced / In Play

- **Ivan Toklas** - routed the job and vouched for it as valuable, standard-looking work.
- **Mr. Johnson** - extremely anonymous, high-end corporate cutout; likely operating for a serious client, but the represented party remains unknown.
- **Cindy Lou Jenkins** - consulted on pre-departure checks and joined the local Matrix legwork as Mevin's social-pattern helper.
- **Claude August** - discussed as the secretly draconic CEO of Princeps, part of the broader corporate-pressure context rather than confirmed job sponsor.

## Clues Gained

- The current target is probably the **Pixel Sticks**, a Matrix youth gang tagging recent vandalism across the Nashville telecom grid.
- The Pixel Sticks appear to have outside backing because they are using utilities far beyond what ordinary script kiddies or local teen deckers should possess.
- The dossier has suspicious blank spots, implying the Johnson or the Johnson's source may be hiding who supplied the tools, who is protected, or what activity would identify the client.
- **Happy Cat Konpeki and Collectibles** was notable because it was repeatedly spared during vandalism waves that hit comparable targets.
- The area around Happy Cat is crowded enough that the team cannot simply identify the Pixel Sticks by looking for teen deckers.
- A powerful awakened animal is present inside Happy Cat.

## Decisions Made

- The crew accepted the contract rather than over-interrogating the Johnson.
- Mevin performed due diligence with Ivan before leaving Atlas's.
- Kurgan checked for a tail and then deliberately left in a way that made the team appear casual and hard to follow.
- The team chose a drive-by and street-level approach at Happy Cat before committing to deeper Matrix action.
- Mevin brought Valgaut into the local Matrix while Kurgan remained outside as physical overwatch.
- Mevin asked Cindy Lou to help profile the local crowd and Matrix traffic.

## Changes to Campaign State

- The active run is now the **Pixel Sticks / bleeding-edge utilities** job.
- The prior **Griswell / Alvin / Palermo** run is fully in the recent-history bucket rather than the active dashboard focus.
- Happy Cat is now a live investigation site, not just a map landmark.
- The local Nashville Matrix youth-gang scene is now an active campaign surface.
- Cindy's role as Mevin's in-session Matrix/social-analysis partner has become explicit at the table.
