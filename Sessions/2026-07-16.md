# Session 2026-07-16

## Summary

The crew continued the **Pixel Sticks / military-grade utilities** investigation at **Happy Cat Konpeki and Collectibles**. **Mevin** entered the Happy Cat host through the Decker Experience and found much more than a cute corner-store storefront: a hidden **Pixel Sticks tag yard** with utility crates, route maps, dead drops, burner personas, reputation tags, and jobs sorted by risk, payout, sponsor, and bragging rights. In meatspace, **Curtis** used **Belmont** to spot suspicious rooftop communications equipment, **Valgaut** and **Kurgan** recognized the awakened creature inside the store as **Chunky Sparkles**, and the crew traced the likely physical infrastructure into the adjacent **Vernon Winfrey arkoblock**.

The session ended with all four runners bypassing public elevator access, taking the stairs through the arkoblock, defeating or slipping past improvised barricades, and stacking up at the **21st-floor landing** near the suspected Pixel Sticks floors.

## Major Scenes

- The table resumed at Happy Cat, a crowded East Nashville convenience-store / collectibles shop at the base of a large residential arkoblock.
- **Mevin** jacked into the Happy Cat host while the rest of the crew observed the physical scene from **Grandpa** and nearby street positions.
- **Curtis** launched **Belmont** to check the roof for hidden gear. He found no weapons cache, but he did spot an odd roof-mounted equipment assembly that none of the non-deckers could immediately identify.
- Curtis preserved still images from Belmont's feed so Mevin could review them after coming out of full Matrix immersion.
- **Valgaut** focused on the awakened-animal presence sensed during the prior session and entered Happy Cat with Curtis and Kurgan.
- A group of hostile youths demanded a **100¥ membership** to enter the shop. Curtis paid rather than start a fight in the crowded storefront.
- Inside, the crew saw a bright, trendy, noisy hangout full of collectibles, digital displays, local youths, and prominent Pixel Sticks iconography.
- The awakened creature inside proved to be **Chunky Sparkles**, a pony-sized, rainbow-maned, winged and horned creature Kurgan recognized from an older unresolved promise to look after it.
- Valgaut's magical read found multiple awakened-animal signatures folded into one creature: at minimum, the impression of an awakened horse and an awakened bird in a single body.
- Mevin found the **Bypass Key** in the Happy Cat host's cluttered manager's office, then reached the hidden Pixel Sticks tag yard behind the cheerful retail sculpting.
- Mevin also gained **camera network access** during the host run.
- The hidden yard looked disciplined and operational, not like a prank board: tag fragments, utility crates, dead-drop lockers, route maps, burner personas, and reputation tags were organized as a working Matrix-gang staging area.
- Curtis used Belmont's roof images to identify a **laser modem** connection between the Happy Cat roof and a blacked-out window in the neighboring arkoblock.
- Through the receiving window, the crew saw electronics consistent with server racks or Matrix infrastructure.
- Mevin confirmed that interrupting the laser line would be physically easy, but his analysis raised a more complicated possibility: the crew might be seeing traffic routed through a civilian jackpoint in the building to anonymize activity from the convenience store.
- Mevin's Matrix intrusion tripped a server shutdown. As the crew left, Valgaut and Curtis overheard a young woman's voice say that someone had gotten all the way in, triggered the shutdown, and that they were checking for a trace.
- The GM confirmed that Mevin was not tagged, his jackpoint was not traced, and he got away clean.
- The crew debated kicking in the store door, intercepting the laser, posing as delivery or tech support, or going directly into the arkoblock.
- Concerned that Chunky Sparkles might react to hostile intent, the crew tested whether it could sense aggressive thoughts or sub-vocal planning from outside the store. No reaction occurred away from the shop interior.
- In the arkoblock atrium, Kurgan recognized Pixel Sticks tags on the **20th, 21st, and 22nd floors**, along with at least one other unknown gang tag.
- The public elevators to those floors were being watched or blocked by hostile-looking youths, so the crew chose the stairwell rather than a direct elevator confrontation.
- Curtis managed one flight of stairs on his own, then rode on Kurgan's shoulders for the long climb while **Mr. Clean** accompanied the team.
- Around the 10th floor, the crew found an improvised barricade meant to filter out transients. Attempts to disassemble and replace it went poorly after Curtis critically failed, leaving an open gap behind them.
- Higher up, Mevin identified several DIY electronic security systems on floor doors, likely installed by criminal or self-defense groups.
- Near the 20th floor, the crew found a stronger sealed barricade. Curtis kicked open a weak low corner, and Mr. Clean crawled through to unlatch it from the inside.
- Past that barricade, the 20th-floor approach looked dusty and unused; Kurgan spotted four strange silver discs going up the stairs.
- The crew advanced stealthily to the **21st-floor landing** and paused there, stacked and ready to enter next session.

## NPCs Introduced / In Play

- **Chunky Sparkles** - awakened, pony-sized, rainbow-maned, winged and horned creature being kept or tolerated inside Happy Cat; magically alarming, but not immediately hostile.
- **Unnamed young woman / Pixel Sticks-side operator** - authoritative voice heard after the server shutdown, directing a trace check.
- **Hostile Happy Cat youths** - local gatekeepers who demanded 100¥ membership and later deferred to the young woman.

## Clues Gained

- Happy Cat is not just a spared landmark; its host hides a disciplined Pixel Sticks staging area.
- The Pixel Sticks hidden yard contains operational structures: utility crates, route maps, dead drops, burner personas, reputation tags, and job organization.
- The visible physical link is a laser modem between Happy Cat's roof and a blacked-out window in the neighboring arkoblock.
- The receiving arkoblock room contains server racks or related Matrix infrastructure.
- The connection may route through a civilian access point or jackpoint, potentially anonymizing activity from Happy Cat through ordinary residential infrastructure.
- The Pixel Sticks appear to claim or control activity around the 20th-22nd floors, though at least one unknown non-decker gang or criminal group also shares the space.
- Chunky Sparkles may respond to hostile intent or magical cues inside Happy Cat, but did not react to the crew's test from the arkoblock atrium.
- Mevin got out clean despite triggering a server shutdown; no trace or jackpoint tag was confirmed.
- The 20th-floor side of the stairwell is dusty enough that no one appears to have passed through for weeks.
- Four strange silver discs were visible up the stairs beyond the 20th-floor barricade.

## Decisions Made

- Curtis paid the 100¥ shakedown rather than starting a fight in Happy Cat.
- Mevin continued deep enough in the Happy Cat host to recover the bypass key, access cameras, and find the hidden Pixel Sticks yard.
- The crew chose not to immediately block the laser modem, since doing so would warn the opposition without necessarily proving enough.
- The crew chose the arkoblock stairwell over a direct confrontation with elevator guards.
- Mr. Clean was used to open the sealed 20th-floor barricade from the inside after Curtis created a low access gap.
- The crew advanced quietly and ended at the 21st-floor door rather than forcing an immediate breach at the end of the night.

## Changes to Campaign State

- The **Pixel Sticks / Military-Grade Utilities Run** has moved from investigation-at-Happy-Cat into an active physical approach on the Pixel Sticks' suspected arkoblock floors.
- The hidden Pixel Sticks tag yard is now discovered in play.
- **Chunky Sparkles** is now an active, visible element at Happy Cat.
- The **Vernon Winfrey arkoblock** is now a named investigation site tied to Happy Cat, the laser modem, and the suspected Pixel Sticks infrastructure.
- Mevin's Happy Cat host intrusion is known to the opposition because it triggered a server shutdown, but Mevin himself was not traced.
