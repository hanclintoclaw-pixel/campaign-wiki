# Session 2026-05-21

## Summary

Picking up at the threshold of **Core 7 / Site 7**, the crew chose talk before assault. Curtis pushed through the receiving-room door under the pretense of a special fish delivery and discovered that **Core 7** was not merely an abstract machine voice: the visible core of the system was a heavily cybered dolphin suspended in a life-support tank, with the apparent host-process interface translating animal drives into eerie pseudo-corporate speech.

Through a tense improvised negotiation, the runners learned that the dolphin intelligence had likely been trapped in this system for roughly **twelve years**, that it could not simply leave without major technical support, and that its immediate obsession with fish was real rather than metaphorical. Mevin's analysis shifted the team's read of Core 7 from "true autonomous AI mastermind" to something closer to a **wetware personality-emulation system**: a high-end machine layer wrapped around a living, awakened marine mind.

That reframed the whole operation. The team concluded that **Mother Yahweh** and the warehouse-side counterfeit-food operation were not independent mysteries but part of the same ugly ecosystem. Core 7 appeared to be hijacking or amplifying human influence channels to bring in fish, while Mother Yahweh was scaling the operation through tainted food, insect-spirit contamination, and the labor of low-level warehouse muscle.

Instead of walking away, the runners looped back to the warehouse itself. There they confirmed the worst version of the theory: inside, Mother Yahweh's people had a grotesque processing line built around a chained, oversized **roach spirit**, literally carving material off the spirit, grinding it into the counterfeit **Meatlets**, and repackaging the product for distribution. Kurgan breached fast, Valgaut destroyed the trapped spirit before it could fully break loose, Curtis brought drone support to bear, Mother Yahweh was dropped in the interior confrontation, and the remaining warehouse workers fled. The session ended with the warehouse-side operation broadly broken open, Core 7 still unresolved in the woods, and the crew now facing not just a mystery but a cleanup problem.

- Core 7's comments and the crew's deductions tied the site more tightly to **Mother Yahweh** and the counterfeit-food pipeline.
- The party withdrew from Site 7, repositioned at the warehouse, and reassessed the relationship between the dolphin system, the tainted food, and the witch running the warehouse layer.

- Kurgan pushed through the interior corridor, Valgaut hacked apart the trapped spirit, Curtis used drone support to stabilize the fight, and Mother Yahweh was taken down while fleeing help arrived too late to matter.
- The remaining low-level warehouse hands broke and ran, leaving the runners in control of the site by session end.

## Clues Gained

- **Core 7** is likely not a clean autonomous machine mind; it behaves more like a **human-readable machine wrapper around a living dolphin intelligence**.
- The dolphin intelligence has likely been trapped in the system for roughly **twelve years**.
- The dolphin cannot simply be removed from Site 7 without technical, medical, and probably magical support.
- The fish obsession is genuine and appears to be one of the key motivational engines behind the influence behavior radiating out from Site 7.
- **Mother Yahweh** and the Site 7 system are functionally linked, whether as collaborators, opportunists, or mutually exploiting adjacent pieces of the same infrastructure.
- The warehouse interior was actively processing counterfeit **Meatlets** using carved-off material from a chained **roach spirit**.
- The counterfeit-food operation was therefore not only tainted or magically influenced but directly dependent on **ongoing insect-spirit exploitation**.
- The warehouse still relied on a throttled generator and dimmed production environment shortly before dawn, suggesting the site was in temporary low-output mode rather than abandoned.
- The remaining warehouse workers appeared to be low-level labor or gangers, not the true strategic brains of the operation.
- The crew now has enough evidence to treat the Darla/Bebop tainted-goods network, Mother Yahweh, and Core 7 as a single interlocked conspiracy rather than separate weird incidents.

## Decisions Made

- Attempt contact and negotiation with Core 7 before launching a direct assault.
- Treat the cybered dolphin as a potentially trapped intelligence rather than immediately as a kill target.
- Promise future fish support / possible rescue rather than forcing extraction on the spot.
- Return to the warehouse instead of leaving the operation intact after the Site 7 conversation.
- Breach the warehouse once the team concluded the tainted-food line and Mother Yahweh remained an immediate threat.
- Prioritize disruption of the spirit-harvesting operation over quietly cashing out the original job and walking away.
- Take down Mother Yahweh's side of the operation without wasting time on a prolonged standoff with the fleeing rank-and-file labor.

## Changes to Campaign State
