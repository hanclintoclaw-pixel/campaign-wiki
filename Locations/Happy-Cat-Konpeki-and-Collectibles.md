# Happy Cat Konpeki and Collectibles

## Overview

Independent convenience store and collectibles shop on Vernon Winfrey Avenue in Nashville. Happy Cat Konpeki and Collectibles has become a neighborhood hangout for local teens: part snack stop, part trading-card counter, part place to loiter where the clerks know when to pretend not to notice.

The store is small, bright, and stubbornly local, nestled between the huge arkoblocks that rise around it. Against all that concrete mass, Happy Cat reads like a pocket of neon clutter and teenage noise that the surrounding towers never quite managed to swallow. Its host and roofline now tie directly into the Pixel Sticks investigation.

## Known Facts

- Independent convenience store and collectibles shop.
- Local teen hangout, with enough foot traffic to feel like an informal neighborhood commons.
- Surrounded by massive arkoblocks, making it stand out as a smaller independent business in the shadow of denser urban infrastructure.
- Useful landmark for street-level activity in the Vernon Winfrey Avenue area.
- During the Pixel Sticks investigation, the store sat inside a dense street scene of teens, tired workers, SINless residents, corporate-fashion posers, and public Matrix users.
- The local Matrix view includes a [Happy Cat Public Storefront Host](../Tech/Matrix/Happy-Cat-Public-Storefront-Host.md) among other nearby storefront hosts.
- The vandalism pattern around the current Matrix-youth-gang problem appears to make Happy Cat a notable exception or investigation point rather than just another hit site.
- Valgaut sensed a fairly powerful awakened animal in or near the store during the first drive-by.
- In Session 2026-07-16, the crew identified that creature as [Chunky Sparkles](../NPCs/Chunky-Sparkles.md), a pony-sized, winged, horned, rainbow-maned awakened creature Kurgan recognized from an older unresolved promise.
- A group of hostile youths demanded **100¥ membership** from the crew before letting them inside.
- The interior is bright, trendy, and noisy, with collectibles, digital displays, and 20-30 youths or young gang-adjacent locals.
- Mevin's host intrusion revealed a hidden Pixel Sticks tag yard behind the public storefront host.
- The roof has a laser modem link to a blacked-out window in the neighboring [Vernon Winfrey Arkoblock](Vernon-Winfrey-Arkoblock.md).
- After Mevin tripped a server shutdown, the store pulled a shutter and an authoritative young woman's voice ordered a trace check; Mevin was not traced.
- The 21st-floor breach confirmed the Happy Cat host / laser-array path nested into the upper-floor Pixel Sticks host infrastructure.
- Names and handles tied to the Happy Cat / Pixel Sticks path now include Highbrow, Handy-Sandy, Pop Onomata, Ernie Pollock, Ziggy Hill, and Lizzo Wells.
