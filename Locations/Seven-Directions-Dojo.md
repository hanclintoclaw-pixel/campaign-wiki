# Seven Directions Dojo

## Overview

**Seven Directions Dojo** is a small civilian martial-arts and wellness school in Nashville, publicly known as a practical self-defense studio with an old-fashioned code-of-conduct streak. It serves ordinary students, off-duty security personnel, and parents looking for disciplined after-school training for their children.

Behind the legitimate business, the building is also the cover site for a hidden Hidden Enforcers facility: [the Oath Floor](Hidden-Enforcers-Oath-Floor.md).

## Public identity

- **Full public name:** Seven Directions Martial Arts & Wellness
- **Neighborhood:** Edge of East Nashville, in a renovated pre-Crash light-industrial block near auto shops, rehearsal spaces, and small churches.
- **Owner of record:** **Maribel Soto**, a retired Lone Star close-protection trainer and licensed physical therapist.
- **Head instructor:** **Elias Crowe**, a quiet physical adept who teaches restraint-heavy defensive forms and keeps the school deliberately unflashy.
- **Public services:** Adult self-defense, youth discipline classes, baton retention seminars, basic first aid, and low-cost mobility rehab for injured workers.
- **Public reputation:** Respectable, a little stern, and unusually good at keeping trouble off the sidewalk without calling attention to itself.

## Description

The front half of Seven Directions looks like a clean, slightly underfunded civilian dojo: scuffed hardwood, white mats, cheap lockers, hand-painted calligraphy, and a front counter that still uses a paper sign-in sheet when the local grid gets flaky.

The building's age gives the cover story texture. Old freight access, sealed maintenance spaces, and reinforced basement walls are explained away as leftover industrial infrastructure. Most visitors never see anything past the changing rooms, therapy office, storage cages, and narrow back hallway.

## Known details

- The school discourages flash, tournament culture, and public challenge matches.
- Students are taught to de-escalate first and leave cleanly when possible.
- Several regular adult students are current or former police, court security, private security, or emergency services personnel.
- The dojo occasionally hosts closed-door "professional safety seminars" after normal hours.
- The back office keeps old paper membership records that do not perfectly match the public scheduling system.
- A locked stairwell behind the mat-storage wall is presented as access to a condemned basement and storm-shelter space.

## Security / access

- During public hours, security is low-key: cameras, strong locks, attentive instructors, and a culture where strangers are noticed quickly.
- After hours, the building is much harder than it looks: shutters, interior maglocks, redundant alarm paths, and concealed chokepoints around the back hall.
- The public basement explanation is plausible enough for inspectors and neighbors, but the lower access is not casual; guests must be brought down by someone trusted.

## Connected people and groups

- Cover site for the Hidden Enforcers' [Oath Floor](Hidden-Enforcers-Oath-Floor.md).
- Publicly connected to Nashville law-enforcement-adjacent circles through alumni, seminars, and quiet referrals.
- Potentially relevant to **[Valgaut](../PCs/Valgaut.md)** if his Hidden Enforcers contact develops into formal initiatory business.
