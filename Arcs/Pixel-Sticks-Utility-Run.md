# Pixel Sticks / Military-Grade Utilities Run

## Current State

The crew has accepted a deniable Matrix cleanup contract routed through **Ivan** at **Atlas's**. The stated target is a troublesome Matrix youth gang causing disruption across the Nashville telecom grid. The first identified tag is **Pixel Sticks**, but the deeper issue is not ordinary vandalism: someone has armed the group with cutting-edge utilities far beyond normal youth-gang capacity.

The paid run is resolved but the fallout is not. In [Session 2026-07-23](../Sessions/2026-07-23.md), the crew breached the [Vernon Winfrey Arkoblock](../Locations/Vernon-Winfrey-Arkoblock.md) 21st-floor Pixel Sticks space, disrupted the cell, recovered used utilities, and received the full **60,000¥** payout from the Johnson-side handoff.

The messy leftovers are now the real story: Mevin retained encrypted utility/paydata material for later work with Cindy Lou, the crew added themselves to a high-end scorpion combat drone's whitelist before triggering it as escape-cover, and that drone was last seen fighting Chunky Sparkles during the crew's elevator escape.

## Known Contract Terms

- **Base pay:** 40,000 nuyen to shut down the threat and remove the group as a concern on the Nashville Matrix.
- **Recovery bonus:** 20,000 nuyen for timely return of the state-of-the-art utilities.
- **Final payout:** 60,000 nuyen total paid after Session 2026-07-23, split 15,000 nuyen each among Mevin, Valgaut, Kurgan, and Curtis.
- **Client:** unknown; represented by an extremely bland, high-quality Mr. Johnson.
- **Broker:** Ivan Toklas at Atlas's.
- **Timeline:** open-ended enough not to force an immediate deadline, but the Johnson implied timeliness matters.

## Known Facts

- Nashville has seen a recent rise in Matrix youth-gang noise and vandalism.
- Pixel Sticks tags have appeared during the last two weeks.
- The group's work includes neon-signature Matrix vandalism, defacement, and nuisance attacks.
- The high-end utilities make the vandalism more dangerous than normal script-kiddie activity.
- Mevin recognized that high-rating bespoke utilities should be extremely valuable if fresh, so the low recovery bonus is suspicious.
- The tools may already be degraded by repeated public use, or the client may simply want evidence that they have been destroyed or recovered.
- The dossier given to the team appears edited; Mevin spotted monitoring gaps where activity should exist.
- The client may be using the crew as deniable assets because the tools, the gang, or the missing data could trace back to them.
- The Happy Cat host contains a hidden Pixel Sticks tag yard with utility crates, dead drops, route maps, burner personas, reputation tags, and job organization.
- A laser modem links Happy Cat's roof to a blacked-out window in the neighboring arkoblock, where the crew saw electronics consistent with server racks or Matrix infrastructure.
- Mevin's host intrusion tripped a server shutdown, but he was not traced or tagged.
- The 21st-floor cell used handles including Highbrow, Handy-Sandy, and Pop Onomata.
- Names on the scorpion drone whitelist included Ernie Pollock, Ziggy Hill, Lizzo Wells, and two goons.
- The upper-floor host terminal confirmed the physical side was nested through the Happy Cat host / laser-array path.
- The used utilities were delivered for the recovery bonus, but Mevin retained encrypted utility/paydata material from the other room.
- The cell had access to a high-end scorpion-like combat drone with a dangerous whitelist/go-nuts mode.

## Active Leads

- Evaluate or crack Mevin's retained encrypted utility/paydata material.
- Track the [Pixel Sticks Scorpion Drone](../Vehicles/Pixel-Sticks-Scorpion-Drone.md), if it survived.
- Determine whether **Chunky Sparkles** survived and whether the crew is now on its bad side.
- Identify the supplier/sponsor behind the utilities and drone hardware.
- Recover what was removed from the Johnson's dossier.
- Preserve caution around proof-of-work, client identity, and whether the Johnson got everything they expected.
