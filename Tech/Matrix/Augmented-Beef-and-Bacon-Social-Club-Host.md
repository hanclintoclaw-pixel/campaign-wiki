# Augmented Beef and Bacon Social Club Host

## Overview

A branded corporate host for a soy-meat company selling artificial beef-and-bacon lifestyle products through marketing, subscription culture, and aggressively engineered consumer identity.

- **Host Color / Security:** Orange-4

## What deckers find inside

- product formulas, flavor-profile research, and supplier records
- advertising plans, loyalty-club data, and influencer partnership files
- subscription fulfillment logistics and customer preference analytics
- test-market studies built around aspiration, masculinity theater, and novelty consumption
- internal strategy files on brand narratives and manufactured authenticity

## Host feel

Ridiculous on purpose, but still dangerous. The host feels like a private meat club crossed with a corporate theme park, all mahogany smokehouse aesthetics and engineered appetite cues.

A smart decker will notice that beneath the joke branding sits a serious consumer-data operation.

## Iconography

- menu trees appear as **wooden club plaques, butcher-block counters, and neon smokehouse signs**
- data archives appear as **cold lockers, recipe books, branded crates, and chrome pantry vaults**
- IC manifests as **chefs, pit bosses, livestock judges, and smiling mascots gone predatory**
- black IC or aggressive trace elements might emerge as **industrial slaughter-line machinery**
- executive spaces resemble exclusive cigar-bar dining rooms where every surface is an ad

## Decker use

Useful for spoof-product scandals, tracing shell vendors, finding marketing leverage, and discovering whether a seemingly silly consumer brand is financing something much uglier.
