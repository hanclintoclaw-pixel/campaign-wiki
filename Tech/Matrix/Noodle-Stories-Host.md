# Noodle Stories Host

## Overview

The **Noodle Stories Host** is the modest public Matrix presence and back-room coordination system for [Noodle Stories](../../Locations/Noodle-Stories.md), a small Nashville arts-district noodle shop tied to the [Noodle Stories Collective](../../Organizations/Noodle-Stories-Collective.md). It is not built like a corporate vault. It is a mid-level **Blue host** whose real defense is low profile, sparse data retention, analog habits, and regulars who notice when something feels wrong.

This page is a wiki Host record only. No machine-readable Decker Experience profile has been created yet.

## SR3 Rules Model

Use normal SR3 Matrix host assumptions:

- Treat the system as a **Blue-5 host** with varied subsystem ratings.
- Decker operations use the relevant subsystem rating as the base target number, adjusted by access route, alert state, legitimate credentials, utilities, and GM modifiers.
- Passive alert raises subsystem ratings by +2 for users in the system, per *Matrix* alert guidance.
- Blue systems should lean toward light sheaf pressure, reactive/proactive White IC, trace risk, and escalation to human response rather than lethal IC.
- Host shutdown is possible at a configured Security Tally threshold; anyone still online when shutdown completes is dumped and resolves dump shock per SR3.
- Paydata should fit the host's nature. This host can contain small, locally valuable data, but it should not be treated as a corporate treasure chest.

## What deckers find inside

Public routes expose ordinary shop-facing material:

- menu items, hours, specials, and public event announcements;
- old poetry-night titles, sanitized zine tables, and public broadsides;
- minimal contact forms that route to staff rather than keeping rich customer data;
- deliberately inconsistent public metadata, partly from neglect and partly from Auntie Static's habits.

Private or staff-side routes can reveal:

- supplier and delivery records for noodles, tea, paper, and cheap printing supplies;
- comped-meal ledgers and informal aid notes, usually written in mundane shorthand;
- staff schedules and after-hours reading calendars;
- RSVP fragments and back-room invitation lists;
- warning notes about suspicious vans, harassment, venue pressure, or sudden landlord interest;
- coded collective signals embedded in poem titles, flyer serial numbers, menu specials, and spoken-word refrains;
- limited camera or door-chime access, with exact physical scope subject to GM confirmation.

## Host feel

The public sculpting looks like a cluttered noodle counter after midnight: steam, paper lanterns, hand-lettered specials, receipt tape, zine racks, and open-mic flyers layered over one another. Public directories appear as menu boards and corkboards. Search results flutter out as receipt slips or chapbook pages.

Private surfaces are not grander; they get more practical. Staff-only files look like grease-stained binders, kitchen prep sheets, envelope stacks, and old portable radios. Security warnings manifest as the room going quiet: spoons stop clinking, the stage mic pops, and a dozen anonymous patron icons turn to look at the intruder.

## Iconography

- **Access:** front door curtain, public counter, staff curtain, delivery entrance.
- **Control:** lights, cheap cameras, kitchen order rail, door chime, stage mic, back-room speaker.
- **Index:** corkboard tags, poem titles, flyer dates, menu specials, receipt numbers.
- **Files:** binders, zines, prep sheets, comp ledgers, shoeboxes of handbills.
- **Slave:** camera bulbs, sign lights, kitchen screens, cheap locks, audio gear.
- **Probe IC:** a nosy regular asking why the intruder is in the wrong room.
- **Trace IC:** receipt tape unspooling toward the jackpoint.
- **Scramble IC:** spilled broth, blurred ink, and poems rearranging into useless enjambment.
- **Tar Baby IC:** sticky noodle dough and a jammed staff curtain holding the icon in place.

## Security Sheaf (SR3-style)

Use this as a prepared Blue-host sheaf guideline. Adjust the exact trigger steps if the live table already has a Security Tally in motion.

| Tally | Event |
| ---: | --- |
| 4 | Probe-4 checks the icon and quietly notes persona details. |
| 7 | Trace-4 starts; if ignored, staff or a trusted neighborhood watcher may receive a warning. |
| 10 | Passive Alert: subsystem ratings effectively rise by +2; public icons continue normally. |
| 12 | Scramble-4 targets copied notes, invitation fragments, or file reads; recovered data may become partial/noisy. |
| 14 | Tar Baby-4 attempts to hold the intruder long enough for human response or clean shutdown. |
| 16 | Active staff notification: Mina, Auntie Static, or a trusted regular is alerted that the host has an intruder. |
| 18 | Host shutdown sequence begins; resolve per SR3 host shutdown / dump-shock guidance if the decker remains online. |

This host should feel defensive and community-protective, not murderous. If Black IC appears here, it is evidence of outside tampering or a hidden third-party asset, not ordinary Noodle Stories security.

## Decker use

Useful for learning whether Noodle Stories noticed a person, pressure campaign, raid pattern, suspicious vehicle, landlord probe, or arts-district rumor before it became public. The host can also help a decker identify how the collective encodes warnings, but it should not answer every political or operational question by itself.

Good concrete successes include:

- staff schedule access;
- supplier/delivery pattern access;
- public poetry archive search;
- coded warning-list access;
- back-room RSVP fragment recovery;
- camera/door-chime access, with GM scope confirmation;
- small local paydata around harassment patterns, landlord pressure, or suspicious surveillance behavior.

Permanent changes, such as altering RSVPs, deleting warnings, spoofing a menu signal, changing supply orders, or suppressing camera/door-chime logs, should be explicitly reported to the GM.

## Paydata Guidance

The host may contain **small local paydata**, not corporate-scale loot. Possible finds:

- pattern notes about suspicious vans, plainclothes visits, or arts-district pressure;
- landlord/property-interest rumors tied to redevelopment;
- buyer lists for underground zines or benefit events;
- corroborating evidence that someone is targeting neighborhood dissidents;
- supplier records showing who suddenly stopped delivering or started asking questions.

If using *Matrix* paydata rules, keep the number of Paydata Points modest for a Blue host and make the contents local, time-sensitive, and ethically complicated. Selling this data carelessly may burn vulnerable people.
