# On SC Music and the Locked Door

**2066-05-01**

I went looking for a theft and found a posture.

That is sometimes more useful.

**SC Music** keeps its digital house the way certain wealthy people keep their living rooms: polished where guests can see, heavily arranged around image, and full of doors they hope nobody curious will try.

The visible structure told me plenty before the deeper truth ever showed its face. The host divided itself in all the expected little priesthoods: **masters and media assets**, **contracts and rights**, **campaign analytics and audience shaping**. Money, ownership, and influence all stacked close enough to share perfume.

That alone does not prove guilt. It does tell me what kind of church I had wandered into.

I borrowed myself a modest internal face and moved carefully. Not executive. Not privileged. Just the sort of tolerated worker account meant to pass through the hallways without being remembered. That was enough to get me near the bookkeeping shelves.

And there, in the contracts and transfer clutter, I found the first thing that made my teeth set.

A reference trail. Buried. Dry-looking. The kind of line item meant to die in a spreadsheet and never become a story.

But it touched **Swiftwing**.

Not in the ordinary way a label touches music. Not in the ordinary way rights, masters, or routine business drift through a system. It read like disguised traffic. Sideways traffic. The sort of paper trail that tells you somebody wanted value, identity, or authorship moved without inviting plain-language questions.

That does not give me the full confession. I do not yet have the sealed explanation sitting in my lap with a neat bow on it. What I have is uglier and, in some ways, more convincing: the shape of concealment.

So I followed the thread upward, toward the deeper executive archive where the real justifications and private names tend to hide.

And that is where the house stopped being polite.

The inner archive did not open for me. It resisted in that cool, expensive way certain systems do, as though access itself were an insult. I pressed farther anyway, because there are moments when caution becomes collaboration. Before I could prise the deeper answer loose, something else in that space noticed me.

Not an alarm exactly. Not a dumb wall.

A presence.

A hand on the back of the neck.

I got thrown out before I could put a proper name to it.

So here is the truth as I currently hold it:

- **SC Music and Swiftwing are linked inside the records in a way that feels deliberate and disguised.**
- **The visible paperwork suggests hidden sponsorship, hidden transfer, or hidden ownership logic behind my situation.**
- **The real explanation is almost certainly buried higher up the food chain than the public-facing files.**
- **Something in that host was prepared to protect it.**

I dislike vagueness when the subject is my own stolen self. But I dislike false certainty more.

So I will say only what I can defend:

The books are dirty.
The lie is organized.
And somebody with sharp elbows is still standing watch over the part of the story they do not want me reading.
