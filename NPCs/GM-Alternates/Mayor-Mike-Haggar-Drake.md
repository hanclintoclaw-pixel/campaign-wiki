# Mayor Mike Haggar - Alternate Drake Model

> **GM-facing alternate only:** This page does not replace the current [Mayor Mike Haggar](../Mayor-Mike-Haggar.md) entry. It is a slide-in model for a no-cyber Haggar whose physical legend comes from being a newly revealed or long-hidden bred drake rather than a cybered street samurai.

## Concept

Mayor Mike Haggar is publicly the same civic bruiser, reform mayor, and political principal the crew already knows. Privately, this alternate model makes him a **bred drake physical adept**: no implants, no bioware, no visible chrome, and no need for any cybernetic explanation for why an older politician can still fold a violent room in half.

This version keeps the important campaign texture intact:

- Haggar remains a city-power principal with **Joseph Neumann** as his quiet intermediary.
- The Righteous Haze cleanup remains a test case for rebuilding his deniable outside apparatus.
- His strength is split between two arenas: formal civic legitimacy and the old animal fact that he is very hard to kill.
- The drake truth should be a campaign lever, not a casual reveal. Used well, it explains the folk-myth aura around Haggar without making every prior scene a dragon plot.

## SR3 Drake Compliance Notes

- **Drake type:** bred drake, not true drake.
- **Creation method:** Priority system, built like a PC and then advanced with 120 Karma.
- **Priority spread:** **A Magic, B Race, C Attributes, D Skills, E Resources**.
- **Race:** bred drake. *Dragons of the Sixth World* treats bred drakes as **Priority B for Race**.
- **Magic:** physical adept. Drake PCs who are adepts must assign **Priority A to Magic**.
- **Required flaw:** automatic **6-point Hunted Flaw** with no Build Points gained. For Haggar, the public office and Joseph's machinery partially mask the hunt, but they do not remove it.
- **Cyberware / bioware:** none. This is intentional; drakes suffer double Essence loss / Bio Index from implants, and implants do not function in dracoform.

### Adept powers at chargen

- **Improved Reflexes 1** - the old Haggar speed, now magical rather than wired.
- **Improved Ability: Unarmed Combat 2** - makes the handshake-and-body-slam legend mechanically real.
- **Killing Hands (M)** - lets him hurt things that should not respect mundane fists.
- **Combat Sense 2** - the politician's sixth sense recast as literal combat intuition.
- **Pain Resistance 2** - he keeps smiling after hits that should end the conversation.
- **Mystic Armor 1** - a little of the mayoral invulnerability myth made true.

### Required flaw / narrative pressure

- **Hunted 6:** great dragons, drake hunters, interested magical factions, corporate collectors, and possibly factions that would rather own a mayor than expose one.

For table use, this should not mean assassins every session. It means Haggar's political machine is partly a survival machine. Joseph's calendar, security cordons, staged public appearances, favor economy, and deniable operators all help keep the astral hunters guessing.

## Dracoform Use

Use the bred drake baseline from *Dragons of the Sixth World* as the dracoform chassis, with Haggar's adept powers layered on because drakes with adept powers can use them in dracoform.

### Bred drake baseline

- **Body:** 7
- **Quickness:** 4 x 4 movement multiplier
- **Strength:** 7
- **Reaction:** 4 baseline
- **Initiative:** 4 + 2D6 baseline
- **Combat Pool:** 5 baseline
- **Attack:** 7M, +1 Reach
- **Powers:** Astral Armor, Enhanced Senses, Innate Spell: Flamethrower, Metahuman Form

### Haggar adjusted dracoform notes

- **Initiative:** use 6 + 3D6 if applying Improved Reflexes 1 in dracoform; otherwise fall back to the printed 4 + 2D6 baseline.
- **Close combat:** Unarmed Combat 6, Improved Ability +2, Strength 7, +1 Reach, Killing Hands (M), and a drake natural attack make him a terrifying grappler/brawler.
- **Durability:** Body 7, Astral Armor, Mystic Armor 1, Pain Resistance 2, no Essence loss.
- **Astral state:** dracoform is dual-natured. Human form can astrally perceive and can switch perception with a Simple Action.
- **Transformation:** Metahuman Form requires an Exclusive Complex Action. Clothing and carried gear do not transform and may be damaged or destroyed.

## Gear and Assets

### Personal gear

Keep his personal kit boring and deniable:

- armored executive clothing
- discreet trauma patch / medkit access through staff
- secure mundane commlink / phone equivalents, not implanted
- formal wardrobe built to be discarded if he has to change shape
- no cyberware, no bioware, no implanted comms, no smartlink

### Civic and shadow assets

- Joseph Neumann's scheduling, deniability, and back-channel operation
- mayoral legal counsel and press operation
- emergency-services visibility and city-security relationships
- old street loyalty from people who remember Haggar before the office
- deniable contractors, with Kurgan and Curtis as recent proof of concept
- selective access to drake-safe bolt-holes and magically quiet meeting spaces

## Table Role

### As a political operator

Haggar should be warm, physical, and almost offensively human in public: big handshakes, eye contact, food at meetings, names remembered, favors tracked, funerals attended. The drake secret should sharpen that instead of replacing it. He survives because people like him, fear him, owe him, and underestimate how much of the old animal is still awake.

Use these pools often:

- **Etiquette 7** for civic rooms, churches, public ceremonies, donors, and old neighborhood appearances.
- **Negotiation 6** when he is horse-trading favors, payouts, policy cover, or deniable work.
- **Leadership 5** when the city apparatus needs to move fast.
- **Intimidation 5** when the smile drops and everyone remembers he used to settle problems personally.
- **Nashville Politics 8** when he knows which lever moves which ward, union, police clique, neighborhood elder, or corporate liaison.

### As a physical threat

Do not play him as reckless. Haggar does not transform because a ganger mouths off. He transforms when the scene is already past politics: assassination attempts, spirit threats, containment breaches, betrayals in private rooms, or a moment where saving Nashville matters more than preserving the mask.

When the mask drops, make it fast and ugly:

- He closes distance rather than trading shots.
- He grapples, pins, throws, and breaks lines of fire.
- He tanks pain without theatrics.
- He uses Flamethrower as a shock reveal or area-denial move, not as his default opening.
- He protects witnesses if possible, because every corpse has political consequences.

## Voice and Presentation

Haggar's public voice is the barbecue handshake version of power: warm, rough, memorable, and suspiciously good at making people feel seen. His private voice is quieter. He does not threaten loudly because he does not need to.

Useful tells:

- His handshake is carefully restrained.
- He never wears anything he cannot lose.
- Joseph schedules meetings in places with odd amounts of open floor space.
- He is too calm around paranormal threats for a mundane mayor.
- He hates body scanners less than astral scrutiny.

## Adventure Hooks

- **The hunters found him:** a dragon's agent identifies Haggar as a free bred drake and tries to use the mayoral office as leverage.
- **Joseph knows more than he says:** Joseph has been arranging Haggar's anti-scrying routines for years and may be compromised.
- **Kurgan gets closer:** the Righteous Haze job was not just cleanup; it was a test for whether Kurgan could become part of the mayor's drake-safe outside apparatus.
- **Public crisis, private form:** Haggar must choose between preserving his secret and stopping a threat on camera.
- **Dragon patron offer:** a great dragon offers to make the Hunted problem go away, at the price of making the mayor's independence go away with it.

## Open Questions Before Promotion

- Is Haggar aware of being a drake from the start of the current campaign, or did the first change happen recently?
- Does Joseph know the whole truth, a partial truth, or only the operational symptoms?
- Is Haggar protected by the Draco Foundation, a dragon patron, a local magical group, or only his own machine?
- Should this replace the old cyber-samurai interpretation entirely, or coexist as a deeper hidden layer behind the public legend?
