# Darla Ledue

## Overview

Up-and-coming country music star tied to the crew's current investigation into a hostile influence/manipulation network moving through Nashville's media layers.

## Known Facts

- Darla is central to the current music-industry arc.
- Her debut album master recordings were reported stolen.
- She was housed in an upscale, corporate-owned penthouse apartment with a built-in recording studio.
- A suspicious promotion/surveillance operation around her involved a low-level videographer and an anonymous sponsor.
- By the 612 Wharf sessions, Darla's name, release schedule, and promo ecosystem were tied to counterfeit goods, poster-swap activity, and a wider influence network far beyond ordinary artist marketing.
- The latest Site 7 fallout suggests Darla's public campaign may be sitting downstream of much older knowbot / influence-engine architecture tied to Core 7, Swiftwing, and SC Music.
- By **2026-05-28**, the crew had concluded that her stolen masters were being used inside a wider influence/manipulation pipeline linked to counterfeit **Meatlets**, tainted **Bebop Cola**, and the buried **Core 7 / Site 7** system.
- In the immediate aftermath of the 612 Wharf run, Darla was being sheltered by **Jacinda Hamich** and pushed toward going to ground before the scandal broke fully in public.
- The public-facing fallout appears likely to frame Darla as part of a bizarre promotional collapse rather than exposing the deeper magical and matrix reality behind the operation.

## Relationships

- Linked to **SC Music** through the record-executive angle.
- Linked to **[Jacinda Hamich](Jacinda-Hamich.md)** as the executive now helping cover her flight/concealment.
- Linked to the crew through the current investigation.
- Linked to an anonymous sponsor / agent working through intermediaries.
- Linked indirectly to the 612 Wharf / Site 7 / counterfeit-promo cluster that appears to be amplifying or exploiting her public rollout.
- Linked indirectly to **Core 7 / Site 7** through the stolen-masters / influence-architecture overlap uncovered during the 612 Wharf arc.
