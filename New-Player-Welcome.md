# New Player Welcome

Welcome to **Nashville Shadowrun**: a Shadowrun 3rd Edition campaign about chrome, country music, corporate lies, local weirdness, and the people trying to survive in the cracks between all of it.

You do **not** need to memorize the whole wiki before playing. This page is the quick orientation pass: what kind of game this is, how the table tends to play, and which parts of the wiki are worth skimming first.

## The Pitch

The campaign is set in and around **Nashville in the 2060s**, where megacorporations, music labels, media manipulation, street crews, local politics, Matrix ghosts, and old secrets all tangle together.

The player crew currently includes:

- **Mevin Kitnick** - a corporate decker with a deep relationship to the Matrix side of the campaign
- **Valgaut** - a physical adept who brings spiritual and martial force to the table
- **Kurgan** - a cybered street samurai with growing City Hall and fixer entanglements
- **Curtis** - a dwarf rigger / smuggler with drones, vehicles, logistics, and animal-rescue complications

The campaign often starts from classic shadowrunning jobs - surveillance, extraction, security work, sabotage, investigation - and then follows the consequences when the crew discovers that the job is connected to something bigger.

## What Makes This Table Different

### Nashville is not just backdrop

The setting leans hard into local flavor: clubs, studios, civic politics, malls, riverfront warehouses, touring culture, religious grifts, music-industry power plays, and the weird ways Shadowrun's Sixth World would chew through a Southern media city.

### The campaign remembers things

The wiki is part of play. Session notes, NPC dossiers, factions, locations, clues, and open questions get written down so past choices keep mattering. If a detail is uncertain, it should be marked as uncertain instead of quietly turning into canon.

### Cindy Lou is part character, part tool

**Cindy Lou Jenkins** is an in-world autonomous knowbot with a country-pop past and a decker brain. At the table, Cindy also helps maintain memory, summarize events, track threads, answer rules questions, and sometimes provide in-character commentary when invited.

The short version: the group has an in-fiction AI assistant, and the real table uses that same conceit as campaign infrastructure.

### Shadowrun 3rd Edition is the rules baseline

Rules answers should use **Shadowrun 3rd Edition** sources and SR3-era assumptions. If something comes from another edition, treat it as non-canon unless the GM explicitly imports it.

### The game likes consequences more than clean quest logs

Jobs create enemies, favors, leverage, rumors, heat, and messy obligations. A page being on the wiki does not mean the crew fully understands it. A clue being old does not mean it is dead.

## How We Play

- **Follow the fiction first.** Mechanics matter, but the table usually starts from what the characters are doing and then finds the appropriate SR3 rule.
- **Ask questions out loud.** New-player confusion is normal in Shadowrun. Ask for context, ask what a name means, ask whether something is common knowledge.
- **Treat the wiki as player memory, not omniscience.** Player-safe pages are meant to be skimmable, but characters may still have different knowledge, secrets, or assumptions.
- **Mark uncertainty.** If you add notes later, it is better to write "maybe," "suspected," or "unconfirmed" than to accidentally canonize a guess.
- **Expect cross-thread play.** A Matrix lead, a street contact, a music-business clue, and a combat problem may all turn out to be parts of the same knot.

## Good New-Player Questions

These are always fair game:

- "Do I know this NPC?"
- "Is this public knowledge, crew knowledge, or secret?"
- "What was the last confirmed fact about this lead?"
- "Is this SR3 RAW, a table ruling, or a GM call?"
- "Which wiki page should I read for this?"

## Current Campaign Flavor

Recent play has involved hidden Matrix infrastructure, music-industry influence operations, counterfeit goods, civic corruption, awakened and cybered animals, corporate surveillance, and a warehouse / underwater-site mystery tied to **612 Wharf** and **Core 7**.

That sounds like a lot because it is. Do not worry about catching every thread immediately. Pick one or two active problems, learn the crew relationships, and let the rest come up through play.

## The One-Sentence Version

This is a Nashville-centered Shadowrun 3E campaign where street-level jobs keep exposing deeper media, Matrix, corporate, and local-power conspiracies - and where the table uses a living wiki plus Cindy Lou's in-world AI presence to make the campaign remember what the characters have done.
