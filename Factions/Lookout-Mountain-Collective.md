# Lookout Mountain Collective

## Overview

The **Lookout Mountain Collective** is a Chattanooga-based decker community built around old utility tunnels, tourist infrastructure, rail-and-river logistics, campus ghosts, and the stubborn local habit of hiding serious work behind pretty scenery. They maintain a data haven called **Ruby Falls**, which Mevin has access to.

The Collective is less a gang than a mutual-aid mesh: deckers, archivists, signal smugglers, retired sysops, and a few politically minded weirdos who believe the Matrix should remember what corps, cops, and churches would rather erase.

## What the Crew Knows

- The Collective operates out of Chattanooga and the Lookout Mountain signal ecosystem.
- They maintain **Ruby Falls**, a data haven accessible to [Mevin Kitnick](../PCs/Mevin-Kitnick.md).
- They are protective of sources, handles, and route hygiene.
- Their public-facing culture leans Appalachian, civic-minded, dryly funny, and suspicious of Nashville money.
- They dislike commercial data brokers, corrupt municipal contractors, and anyone who burns community infrastructure for clout.
- They are not automatically hostile to shadowrunners, but they expect courtesy, provenance, and clean hands around shared data.

## Agenda

- Preserve records that powerful institutions try to bury.
- Maintain Ruby Falls as a trusted regional data haven.
- Keep Chattanooga's independent Matrix culture from being eaten by corps, police contractors, or Matrix gangs.
- Trade in verified information rather than rumor spam.
- Help responsible deckers survive long enough to become useful neighbors.

## Resources

- **Ruby Falls data haven:** the Collective's crown jewel and Mevin's access point. Its public-facing Matrix profile is tracked at [Ruby Falls Data Haven Host](../Tech/Matrix/Ruby-Falls-Data-Haven-Host.md).
- A distributed network of local deckers, archivists, and infrastructure watchers.
- Backups of municipal leaks, utility maps, rail schedules, old newsfeeds, and nonprofit/church/civic records.
- Social credibility among some Chattanooga activists, repair crews, and local-grid regulars.
- A habit of routing sensitive traffic through tourist clutter, weather sensors, camera noise, and old maintenance systems.

## Culture and Style

The Collective likes jokes that sound like warnings. Members talk about elevation, caves, switchbacks, flood levels, and rail grades as if those are social principles. Their best operators tend to be patient and local: they know where the fiber crosses the river, which utility contractor lies, and which pretty tourist icon is actually a useful signal blind.

They are friendly enough once trust is established, but they are not casual. A decker who treats Ruby Falls like a public file dump will be corrected once, then excluded.

## Relationships

- **Mevin Kitnick:** has access to Ruby Falls. The exact depth and terms of that access can be developed in play.
- **Palermo:** known to maintain Matrix-facing contact through the Ruby Falls data haven. This does not mean the Collective endorses him; it only confirms Ruby Falls is part of his contact surface.
- **Pixel Sticks:** likely viewed as noisy, reckless, and dangerous if the Collective learns the full extent of the gang's sabotage work.
- **Nashville crew:** not yet a known formal relationship beyond Mevin's access.
- **Local Chattanooga institutions:** the Collective watches them closely, sometimes as citizens, sometimes as intruders, and sometimes as archivists.
