# Hidden Enforcers - True Names

> **True Names / rules-facing:** This page describes the real structure behind the player-facing [Hidden Enforcers](../Hidden-Enforcers.md) stub. It includes out-of-character rules scaffolding for what an SR3 initiatory group looks like in play.

## True Nature

The **Hidden Enforcers** are the covert magical initiatory apparatus serving the Nashville PD sphere: sworn awakened officers, police-adjacent magical specialists, and a small number of trusted former officers or civic-security assets who maintain the magical discipline Nashville law enforcement cannot admit to publicly.

They are not a formal department on paper. They are an umbrella initiatory group with several linked sub-groups, usually called **circles**, **lodges**, or **orders** depending on the members' tradition and internal culture. The public-facing police chain of command and the magical chain of initiation overlap, but they are not identical.

Their practical job is to manage initiation, magical vetting, ritual support, internal discipline, and emergency magical response for law-enforcement magical users in and around Nashville.

## Campaign Role

- **Cover identity:** police-adjacent professional society, informal awakened-officer network, or rumor-level covert order.
- **Actual function:** initiatory group and magical mutual-aid network for Nashville PD-sphere awakened personnel.
- **Known contact point:** [Glenn](../../NPCs/Glenn.md), a high-level hermetic mage and Hidden Enforcers member.
- **Campaign posture:** semi-allied with the crew through prior successful work, but still protective of its secrecy, legitimacy, and police mandate.
- **Operating bias:** civic containment first, public truth second, internal magical stability always.

## Rules Frame

Use **Shadowrun Third Edition** and **Magic in the Shadows** magical group rules as the mechanical basis.

### Group Type

The Hidden Enforcers are best modeled as an **initiatory magical group** with secondary **dedicated** and **conspiratorial** traits.

- **Initiatory:** their core purpose is magical advancement, shared resources, and group initiation.
- **Dedicated:** they have a civic-order / law-enforcement moral code.
- **Conspiratorial:** they are secret, embedded in state power, and willing to use covert authority to protect the group and the city.

This mixed profile is rules-compliant: MitS treats the three group types as broad categories that can overlap when defining a group's nature and strictures.

### Structure: One Group, Many Circles

MitS notes that large magical groups commonly break into smaller sub-groups such as circles, lodges, ranks, or orders. That fits the Hidden Enforcers cleanly.

Working model:

- **The Oath Circle:** senior initiates who maintain the group's magical link, approve members, and adjudicate stricture violations.
- **Precinct Circles:** small local cells built around stations, task forces, or trusted patrol/investigative networks.
- **Special Threats Circle:** members assigned to magical crime, spirits, toxic threats, insect-spirit fallout, and other high-risk occult work.
- **Custody / Evidence Circle:** ritual-security specialists who handle awakened evidence, material links, lodge access, wards, and magically sensitive detainees.

These names are provisional campaign-facing handles. The rules point is that the Hidden Enforcers can be a single initiatory group while still being operationally divided into sub-groups.

## Proposed Strictures

The following strictures fit both MitS and the Nashville PD concept. Treat this as the current rules-compliant draft unless the GM changes it.

### Group Strictures

- **Oath:** members must swear the Enforcer Oath as part of their first initiation with the group. The oath ordeal can reduce initiation cost only when it is the character's first qualifying oath ordeal.
- **Limited Membership:** membership is limited to awakened law-enforcement magical users, sworn civic-security magical assets, or explicitly sponsored former officers / trusted auxiliaries.
- **Material Link:** senior members and full initiates may be required to lodge a thesis, blood sample, hair sample, or equivalent link under sealed internal custody. This is powerful leverage and should be used carefully in play.

### Individual Strictures

- **Secrecy:** members do not publicly admit the group's existence or their membership.
- **Attendance:** members must attend periodic rituals, reviews, or circle meetings. For active-duty members, these may be disguised as training, evidence review, continuing-education, or tactical briefings.
- **Obedience:** members must obey lawful orders from the magical hierarchy when those orders concern group security, magical discipline, initiation, or occult threat response.
- **Fraternity:** members are expected to assist other members when asked, especially in magical emergencies, astral trouble, ritual support, or cleanup operations.
- **Deed:** each member periodically owes service to the group: casework, magical containment, astral overwatch, ritual assistance, recovery of magical evidence, or training younger awakened officers.

### Strictures to Avoid or Use Sparingly

- **Exclusive Membership:** risky for this concept because the group contains several linked sub-groups and may need members with prior tradition-specific ties. Use only if the Hidden Enforcers require approval before joining any outside magical group.
- **Exclusive Ritual:** plausible for internal security, but restrictive for police work where outside magical consultants may be needed.
- **Sacrifice:** mechanically harsh because it reduces Karma awards. Better reserved for an elite internal circle rather than the umbrella group.

## Resources and Patronage

Model the Hidden Enforcers as having **High Resources** in the Nashville area, with access varying by circle.

- **Patron:** Nashville PD sphere / law-enforcement patronage, routed through training budgets, evidence facilities, task-force grants, seized assets, and friendly civic-security relationships.
- **Dues:** low to moderate for ordinary members; much of the cost is absorbed by the patron network.
- **Facilities:** secure ritual rooms, warded evidence storage, libraries, lodges, forensic-magic workspaces, and trusted safe houses.
- **Tradeoff:** patron support means service obligations. The group is not independent of Nashville civic power.

## Joining and Initiation Hooks

Rules touchpoints from MitS:

- Joining a magical group costs Karma and dues, and requires compatibility with the group's magical link.
- Admission can be tested using the applicant's highest Magical Skill or Magic Attribute against the group's current membership, modified by initiate grade.
- Large groups can route applicants into a compatible circle if one branch cannot accept them.
- Members who violate strictures risk trouble at later initiation; serious violations can force expulsion or endanger the group's magical link.
- If the group itself violates a group stricture, it can lose its magical link and must restore or rebuild that link before members can benefit from group initiation.

For the Hidden Enforcers, a PC or NPC usually does not simply apply. They are noticed, investigated, quietly approached, and then tested through service.

## Avatar / Magical Link

The group must have a magical link to function as an initiatory group. The exact avatar should stay undefined until needed in play.

Possible interpretations:

- **The Badge:** civic authority, duty, and mutual recognition.
- **The Blind Witness:** justice, evidence, and hidden truth.
- **The Blue Lantern:** watchfulness, containment, and guidance through darkness.

The avatar should not be treated as a literal police spirit by default. It is better as a liminal symbol: law, secrecy, service, guilt, and the burden of seeing what mundane officers cannot.

## Internal Tensions

- **Law vs. secrecy:** members enforce law while hiding an unlawful or unacknowledged magical command structure.
- **Police duty vs. magical oath:** mundane orders and initiatory obligations can conflict.
- **Public safety vs. civil rights:** evidence gathered magically, especially astrally or through material links, is politically dangerous.
- **Initiatory advancement vs. public service:** the group can justify risky fieldwork as service, but ambitious initiates may exploit the badge to climb grades.
- **Circle autonomy:** sub-groups may disagree on whether the Hidden Enforcers are guardians, secret police, magical internal affairs, or a civic occult fraternity.

## Use at the Table

Use the Hidden Enforcers when the story needs:

- a legitimate-but-hidden magical authority in Nashville;
- a path for law-enforcement awakened NPCs to initiate;
- magical backup that comes with strings;
- internal police occult politics;
- a reason Glenn can recognize, signal, or access police-grade magical assets;
- a rules-compliant structure for initiation, strictures, dues, resources, and magical group discipline.

## Open GM Questions

- What is the group's actual avatar or magical-link symbol?
- Does Glenn belong to the Oath Circle, a specialist circle, or a looser allied circle?
- Are adepts full members, auxiliary members, or routed into their own circle?
- How much of Nashville PD command knows the group exists?
- Has the group ever lost or nearly lost its magical link because of corruption, coverups, or unlawful violence?
